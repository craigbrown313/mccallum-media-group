Common Questions

What pastors
usually ask.

We're a smaller church. Is this for us?+
Yes. We've designed packages that scale — the sermon-to-social engine works the same for a 300-attendance church as a 3,000-attendance one. The pricing scales with the volume of content you need, not the size of your campus.
Do we need to send you our raw sermon files?+
Just the master upload (the same file you'd put on YouTube). We work from there. Most churches send a shared drive link or upload weekly to a folder we set up. The whole intake is built to be three minutes of effort on your end.
Will the content reflect our theology?+
Yes — and this matters to us. We pull pull-quotes and clips that match your tradition's tone and theological emphasis. We won't pick the “most clickable” clip if it misrepresents what was actually said. We err toward what's true to the message every time.
Can we get cuts ready by Monday morning?+
Yes — that's the typical delivery window. Sunday sermon in by Sunday night, finished cuts in your hands by Monday end-of-day. Some packages include same-day rush for Sunday-afternoon delivery on key moments.
Will this replace our comms team?+
No — it equips them. Most of our church clients have a 1–3 person comms team. We become the engine for the video work specifically, which gives them back the hours they need to focus on the strategy, the bulletin, the events, the discipleship content. The team gets stronger, not replaced.
Do you handle livestream production?+
Not directly — that's its own discipline best handled in-house or by a livestream specialist. But we can advise on workflows that get your livestream master into a post-production pipeline cleanly, and we have partners we trust if you need a referral.

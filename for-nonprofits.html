Common Questions

What nonprofits
usually ask.

We're a small org. Can you actually fit our budget?+
Yes. We have packages that start meaningfully below traditional agency rates, and we structure projects so a single production day fuels months of content. We're honest in the first call about whether your budget and goals match — we don't take projects we can't do well.
We work with sensitive populations. Can you handle that?+
Yes. We have experience with trauma-informed production, consent workflows, identity protection (silhouettes, voice modulation, composite stories where appropriate), and on-set sensitivity protocols. Beneficiary dignity is non-negotiable on our shoots.
Do you offer pro bono or discounted nonprofit rates?+
We reserve a small portion of our annual calendar for cause-aligned pro bono and reduced-rate work. Reach out and tell us about the mission — if it fits, we'll find a way.
Can the film be ready for our event in 6 weeks?+
Often, yes — depending on scope. A focused 3-minute impact film with one shoot day can absolutely turn in 6 weeks. A multi-location documentary, no. We'll tell you the truth on the first call.
Will we own the final film?+
Yes. You own the finished deliverables outright with full rights to distribute on any platform you choose. We retain rights to use our work in our portfolio — with your approval on case studies.
Do you provide deliverables for grant applications?+
Absolutely. Grant-ready video deliverables, impact report assets, board update films, and stakeholder cuts are common formats we build for. Tell us the requirements up front and we'll match them.
